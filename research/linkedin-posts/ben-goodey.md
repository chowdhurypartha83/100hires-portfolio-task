\## Post 1 — Translation/Localization AI Visibility Study (2 weeks ago)

<https://www.linkedin.com/posts/benjamingoodey_final-results-are-in-does-translating-content-activity-7470807087657324545-7C2p>



Study testing whether translating content gets a brand cited and recommended more by AI. Setup: chose 14 blog posts from a B2B SaaS client (10 product listicles, 4 educational guides), measured baseline metrics, tracked prompts in German/Spanish (with Peec AI), auto-translated URLs with Weglot, and indexed the new blogs with Search Console.



Month 1 results: +102 clicks, +27,797 impressions. Spanish visibility moved from 6% to 23%; German from 4.5% to 16.8%. The brand was recommended 3.83x more in Spain and 3.73x more in Germany after one month.



Month 2: +184 clicks, +47,300 impressions. Spanish visibility climbed to 32%; German held steady around 15%. Total 5.33x visibility increase from day one. Visibility evolved differently by AI model — ChatGPT peaked in month 1 and dropped in month 2, while Perplexity nearly doubled again in month 2, suggesting rankings/clicks correlate more heavily with brand recommendations on some AI models than others.



Disclosure: the research was sponsored by Weglot, but they didn't influence the results, and the agreement was to share results regardless of outcome.

\## Post 2 — Backlink Reverse-Engineering Tactics (3 weeks ago)

<https://www.linkedin.com/posts/benjamingoodey_most-teams-we-work-with-really-struggle-to-activity-7468267941113954304-PuAn>



Most teams struggle to get backlinks, but they're fundamental to how modern search works (backlinks = authority = ranking/visibility). Three link-building tactics that leverage competitor websites for ideas, using a tool like Semrush:



1\. Filter competitor backlink titles for "best" — shows links earned from roundup-style content. Reach out to the highest-authority roundups and offer swaps.

2\. Look at competitor anchor text — sites with keyword-optimized anchor text patterns are likely open to link exchanges and swaps.

3\. Look at competitors' top pages by backlinks — identify content types/topics that naturally earn links, then build a better version and pitch sites to link to yours instead.



Proper brand building and great content do a lot of authority-building organically, but proactive link acquisition moves things faster.

\## Post 3 — Long-Tail Keywords as a Proxy for LLM Search Behavior (1 month ago)

<https://www.linkedin.com/posts/benjamingoodey_long-tail-keywords-are-one-of-the-closest-activity-7460669161699049473-Yh3z>



Long-tail keywords are one of the closest proxies for how people search using LLMs. AI search behavior differs fundamentally from traditional keyword search — people ask full questions, follow-ups, and multi-turn queries, much of which is invisible to keyword tools. Still, long-tail keywords remain the closest available proxy.



Simple method using Semrush: enter a software head term, apply a "Word Count" filter (5+ words), look for niche identifiers (e.g., "...software for architects"), save relevant/winnable terms, and create an optimized landing page connecting the product to that specific persona — producing something highly relevant for LLMs to reference and cite. This playbook has worked well to improve AI visibility for particular personas and industries.

\## Post 4 — Site Security and Content Quality Protection Case Study (1 month ago)

<https://www.linkedin.com/posts/benjamingoodey_were-seeing-a-lot-of-big-sites-getting-hammered-activity-7454499702928011265-NVvA>



Big sites are getting hammered lately, losing 90%+ of their traffic within a month — happened to one client, whose WordPress site got hacked and turned into a fake casino due to plugin vulnerabilities. Five ways to protect against this:



1\. Conduct a thorough website security review; remove unnecessary WordPress plugins immediately.

2\. Stay away from AI-generated programmatic SEO projects — short-term traffic gains destroy long-term credibility and trigger spam detection.

3\. Invest in content quality — avoid commodity AI content; bring genuine expertise and insight.

4\. Beware unproven "latest best practices" — including low-quality bottom-of-funnel listicles built purely to influence AEO without serving the customer.

5\. Invest in brand building — the most resilient sites long-term have the most trusted brands, which insulates against traffic loss when things go wrong.
