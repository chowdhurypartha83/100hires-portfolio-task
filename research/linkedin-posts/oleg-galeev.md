\## Post 1 — Reddit AEO Citation Data and Myth-Busting (1 day ago)

<https://www.linkedin.com/posts/ogaleev\_aeo-aisearch-geo-activity-7476008398119936000-QoHr>



Everyone's selling Reddit as the magic AEO hack — "just get brand mentions on Reddit and ChatGPT will cite you." Technically true, also deeply misleading. Reddit accounts for \~10% of AI citations across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews — not nothing, not everything.



That 10% comes with conditions: dropping a brand into a dead thread is worthless, since AI models weight posts from popular, actively upvoted discussions. One mention isn't enough — multiple upvoted comments reinforcing the same entity signal are needed. The Andrew Shotland experiment proved this works, but also that it reverses completely once you stop — it's a tap, not a valve.



Ahrefs analyzed 75,000 brands and found branded web mentions overall had a 0.664 correlation with AI Overview inclusion, far stronger than backlinks. Reddit contributes to that pool, but so does every other platform where people talk about a brand. AEO agencies selling Reddit alone are giving one ingredient and calling it the recipe.

\## Post 2 — Spotting "AI Slop" Patterns in Reddit Marketing Advice (1 month ago)

<https://www.linkedin.com/posts/ogaleev\_linkedin-is-flooded-with-ai-slop-posts-about-activity-7455652040204001280-fCRE>



LinkedIn is flooded with AI slop posts about Reddit marketing, all following identical patterns. Categorized the most common phrases:



\- "Reddit is underrated" hooks — "Most marketers are sleeping on Reddit," "Reddit is a goldmine"

\- The "X first, Y second" formula — "Community first, promotion second," "Value first, promotion second"

\- The fake ratio — "the 90/10 rule," "9 helpful comments for every 1 promo." Reddit officially retired the 90/10 rule years ago, but it lives on in LinkedIn posts because it sounds authoritative — nobody actually counts.

\- Threat clichés — "Reddit will eat you alive," "you'll get downvoted into oblivion"



A pointed, self-aware critique of generic, recycled advice that circulates without anyone actually testing or measuring it.
